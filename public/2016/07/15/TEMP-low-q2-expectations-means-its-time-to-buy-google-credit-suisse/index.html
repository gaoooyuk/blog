Low Q2 Expectations Means It’s Time To Buy Google: Credit Suisse

Author: Teresa Rivas

Source: Barrons

Google parent Alphabet (GOOGL) reports second-quarter earnings on July 28, and Credit Suisse thinks investors will be pleased with the results.

Analyst Stephen Ju and his team reiterated an Outperform rating and $920 price target on the stock, writing that intraquarter, Google shares peak to trough have changed 14%, due to concerns related to tougher comparisons in the second half of 2016, as well as Brexit. Therefore, with investors already looking for weak results, Ju writes that this is an excellent time to initiate or add positions in the stock.

In fact, he believes the second quarter won’t be as soft as many are fearing, given price and volume accelerants—expanded text ads, individual bid adjustments, and the more deployment of the fourth sponsored link on desktop feature.

More detail from the note:

Overall, our investment thesis for GOOGL shares remain the following: 1) continued narrowing of mobile/desktop monetization gap in Int’l combined with eventual higher pricing for mobile CPCs given greater context – through a plethora of products including app install, as well as increased ad load on the mobile and desktop SERP, 2) larger-than-expected contribution from Google’s larger non-search businesses, namely YouTube and Play and eventually GCP, 3) optionality and upward bias to estimates and shareholder value creation from Alphabet’s other bets.

Google is up 1.4% to $737.21 in recent trading.
