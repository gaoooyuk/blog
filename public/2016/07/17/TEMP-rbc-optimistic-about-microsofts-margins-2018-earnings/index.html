RBC Optimistic About Microsoft’s Margins, 2018 Earnings

Author: Teresa Rivas

Source: Barrons

Microsoft (MSFT) reports fourth-quarter earnings on July 19, and while RBC Capital Markets’ Ross MacMillan and his team are still forecasting earnings and revenues just below the Street’s expectations for this quarter and next, they write that they’re encouraged to see expectations coming down to a more reasonable level.

MacMillian reiterated an Outperform rating and $61 price target on the stock, writing that the company’s EPS growth profile is already quite attractive, given that excluding foreign exchange, the company has grown EPS 14% so far in fiscal 2016, “unrivaled” by mega cap peers like Oracle (ORCL) and SAP (SAP). Thus, they think the shares deserve to trade at a premium, especially given their confidence in Microsoft’s ability to sustain growth.

More thoughts from their note:

  • Thinking through Capex and Commercial Cloud Gross Margins: Our deep dive into the cloud capex cycle suggests that capex growth will moderate in FY17/ FY18 (MSFT has already built out 26 of 34 planned geolocation datacenters). Under this assumption (but still assuming higher absolute capex dollars) suggests our FY18 Commercial Cloud GMs could be too conservative. Our math suggests this could realize around $0.15 of incremental EPS relative to our forecasts in FY18.

    ​

  • MSFT’s returns on total capex look good. Bear’s argue the MSFT is spending a lot of incremental capex on Azure relative to what Amazon spends on AWS. But looking at capex holistically for all of MSFT’s cloud assets (Azure, O365, Dynamics CRM, Bing, Xbox Live, etc) suggests very good returns on capital investment. We push back on the bear argument in this note.

They also write that the firm’s LinkedIn (LNKD) consolidation, along with phone restructuring, will lead to higher gross margins:

  • The combination of Phone restructuring (sale of feature phone business and a streamlining of smartphone with ~1850 job cuts and restructuring charge of $950M) and consolidation of LNKD (we assume an end CY16 close) should optically improve gross margins over the next year with nominal impact to EPS.
